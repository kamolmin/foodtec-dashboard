📊 KPI Cards — What They Mean
Top of dashboard
KPIFormulaWhat it tells youTargetAction if red
🎯 Avg Sales Pace Actual to Date ÷ (Forecast × Days Elapsed / Days in Month) × 100 How fast sales are moving vs where they should be TODAY. Automatically adjusts every day — day 14 of 31 = 45% expected. 100% by month end Below 75% → sales team needs to push harder on specific SKUs this week
📉 SKUs Behind Pace Count of SKUs where Pace % < 75% Number of products not selling fast enough relative to today's date. Each one needs a sales conversation. 0 SKUs Call the client for each behind SKU. Understand why. Is it stock or demand?
📦 Coverage Gaps Count where (Warehouse + In-Transit) < May Forecast Products without enough physical stock to fulfill the full monthly forecast. Each gap = potential lost sale. 0 gaps Place purchase order immediately. Check lead time — if >30 days, order now for next month.
💰 Avg Inventory Turnover Revenue 12m ÷ Avg Inventory Value (excludes SKUs ≥8×) How many times per year FoodTec's capital cycles. SKUs ≥8× excluded — they are usually stockouts inflating the number. ≥4× per year Below 4× → identify which SKUs are dragging it down. Reduce excess stock on slow movers.
🚚 Order Fill Rate Shipped ÷ Confirmed Orders × 100 Of everything clients confirmed this week, how much actually shipped? Measures execution reliability. ≥95% Below 95% → was it a stock issue or logistics issue? Two different problems, two different fixes.
⚡ Open Actions Count where Status = Open Total outstanding tasks. Sub-number shows CRITICAL count. High number means team is behind on follow-through. 0 Critical open Review each CRITICAL action in the meeting. Assign owner and due date. No action without an owner.
📑 Tab Pages — How to Read Them
Click any tab at the top
TabWhat it showsKey columns to focus onColor logic
🎯 Monthly Pace Sales progress per SKU vs expected daily pace. Progress bar with black marker = where you should be, colored bar = where you are. Pace %, Gap (units), Gap (USD) 🟢 ≥95% on pace · 🟡 75–94% at risk · 🔴 <75% behind · Black = critical
📦 Coverage For each SKU: warehouse stock + in-transit vs May forecast. Stacked bar shows split between warehouse and in-transit. Coverage %, Gap (units), Notes 🟢 ≥100% covered · 🟡 60–99% partial · 🔴 <60% critical gap
💰 Cash Cycle How many days cash is locked up per SKU (WH + transit + customs + AR ÷ daily consumption). Turnover only shown when SKU is covered. Days Capital Tied, Capital Value (USD), Coverage vs Target 🟢 <90 days · 🟡 90–180 days · 🔴 >180 days · Red alert = order required
🏭 Stock Health Current stock value vs justified target value per SKU. Identifies excess capital and deficits in USD terms. Excess USD, Deficit USD, Aging 60+ USD 🟡 Excess = too much cash locked · 🔴 Deficit = stockout risk · Aging = cash at risk of obsolescence
🚚 Fill Rate Weekly fill rate per SKU. Fill in confirmed orders and shipped quantities each week. Status calculates automatically. Confirmed Orders, Shipped, Fill Rate % 🟢 ≥95% on track · 🟡 70–94% at risk · 🔴 <70% miss
⚡ Actions All open tasks sorted by priority. SC = Supply Chain team. SALES = Sales team. Mark done when complete. Priority, Owner, Due Date, Status 🔴 CRITICAL = act today · 🟡 HIGH = this week · 🔵 MEDIUM = this month · ⚪ LOW = monitor

Monthly Pace formulaToday is day X of Y days in the month. Expected pace = X/Y × Monthly Forecast. Resets automatically on the 1st of each month.
Inventory Turnover filterCompany average excludes SKUs with turnover ≥8× (stockouts and fast outliers that distort the average upward). Target is ≥4× for an importing business with 30–74 day lead times.
Safety bufferAll Target DoH calculations use Lead Time + 7 days safety buffer. This can be adjusted per SKU in the SKU Master tab by updating the Target_DOH_Days column.
Cash Cycle — AR UnitsManual input — units shipped to clients but payment not yet received. Update each Monday from the AR plan file. Will eventually feed automatically from ibox.
Rolling DoH averageFill in Weekly_DOH_Snapshot_1 through _4 in Cash_Cycle tab each Monday. Dashboard calculates rolling average from however many weeks have data.
